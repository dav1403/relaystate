## Two Cities, Two Logics

Jerusalem and Tel Aviv are often presented as the two main options for real estate investment in Israel. Yet they respond to completely different logics.

## Tel Aviv: Israel's Most Liquid Market

Tel Aviv is Israel's economic and cultural capital. Its real estate market is one of the most dynamic in the world.

**Strengths:**
- Maximum liquidity — properties sell fast
- Very tight rental market (rental yield 2.5-4%)
- Strong international demand (tech, finance)
- Globally recognized value

**Weaknesses:**
- Prices among the highest in the world (20,000 to 50,000 ILS/sqm in the center)
- Potential bubble — some analysts are cautious
- Very sensitive to interest rates
- Less neighborhood diversity than Jerusalem

## Jerusalem: The Safe Haven

Jerusalem is a unique city in the world. Its status as a Holy City creates structural demand that transcends economic cycles.

**Strengths:**
- Prices still lower than Tel Aviv (8,000 to 30,000 ILS/sqm depending on neighborhood)
- Constant worldwide diaspora and religious demand
- Less volatility than Tel Aviv
- Strong seasonal and tourist rental demand
- Less saturated market — still undervalued opportunities

**Weaknesses:**
- Lower liquidity (properties take longer to sell)
- More complex market (zones, regulations)
- Political tensions can affect perception

## Which Profile?

**Choose Tel Aviv if:**
- You want maximum liquidity
- You have a short investment horizon (< 5 years)
- You're targeting tech/startup tenants
- You're comfortable with a premium market

**Choose Jerusalem if:**
- You're looking for long-term value
- You have a cultural or religious connection to the city
- You want to target the diaspora (buyers/tenants)
- You're looking for still-accessible prices with potential
