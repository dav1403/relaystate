## What Is the Mas Rechisha?

The **Mas Rechisha** (מס רכישה) is Israel's property acquisition tax. It is paid by the buyer at the time of the transaction. Think of it as a transfer tax or stamp duty.

## Rate for Israeli Residents (First Home)

The scale is progressive:
- Up to 1,978,745 ILS: **0%**
- From 1,978,745 to 2,347,040 ILS: **3.5%**
- From 2,347,040 to 6,055,070 ILS: **5%**
- From 6,055,070 to 20,183,565 ILS: **8%**
- Above: **10%**

These thresholds are updated annually.

## Rate for Non-Residents and Investors

If you're buying a second home or are not an Israeli resident, the scale is very different and much less favorable:

- Up to 6,055,070 ILS: **8%**
- Above: **10%**

**Key point**: Foreign nationals who are not Israeli residents pay the "investor" rate even for their first purchase in Israel.

## The Olim Hadashim Exception

**Olim hadashim** (new immigrants) benefit from a reduced rate during the first 7 years following their aliyah:
- Up to 1,978,745 ILS: **0.5%**
- Above: **5%**

This is a considerable tax advantage that can represent tens of thousands of shekels in savings.

## Practical Calculation

For an apartment at 3,500,000 ILS purchased by a non-resident:
- 3,500,000 × 8% = **280,000 ILS** (approximately $75,000)

For an Israeli resident (first home):
- **Total: approximately 70,538 ILS** (approximately $19,000)

The gap is enormous. This is why many foreign buyers consider making aliyah before purchasing.

## Other Costs to Anticipate

In addition to Mas Rechisha, budget for:
- **Attorney fees**: 0.5% to 1.5% of the price
- **Real estate agency**: 2% + VAT (sometimes split buyer/seller)
- **Moving and furnishing costs**

## How to Reduce the Tax Bill?

1. **Make aliyah** before buying if you plan to settle in Israel
2. **Time your purchase** carefully — if you're in the process of aliyah, the date of first purchase within 7 years is critical
3. **Consult a specialist** in Franco-Israeli or Anglo-Israeli tax matters
