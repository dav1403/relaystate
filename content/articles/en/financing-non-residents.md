## Is It Possible to Get a Loan in Israel Without Residency?

Yes, Israeli banks do grant mortgages to non-residents. But the conditions are significantly stricter than for residents.

## Key Conditions

### Minimum Down Payment

For an Israeli resident buying their first home: 25% minimum.
For a non-resident: **50% minimum** of the property price.

In practice, on a 3,000,000 ILS apartment, you must bring 1,500,000 ILS (approximately $400,000) in own funds.

### Interest Rate

Israeli rates vary according to the Bank of Israel's policy rate. In 2026, they range between 4.5% and 6.5% depending on profile and duration. There are fixed-rate loans (מסלול קבוע) and inflation-linked loans (מסלול צמוד מדד).

**Warning**: Inflation-linked loans may seem cheaper but carry the risk of increasing the capital owed if inflation rises.

### Required Documents

Israeli banks require:
- Valid passport
- Bank statements for the last 12 months
- Income proof (pay slips, tax returns)
- Proof that own funds have existed for at least 90 days

## Which Banks to Work With?

Main Israeli banks that work with non-residents:
- **Bank Hapoalim** — the largest, international presence
- **Bank Leumi** — US and UK offices, used to English-speaking clients
- **Bank Discount** — less well-known but often more flexible

There are also brokers specializing in loans for the Jewish diaspora who can negotiate better conditions.

## Alternatives to an Israeli Loan

### Financing from Your Home Country

Some banks in your home country accept financing a property abroad using a local property as collateral. If you have equity in real estate at home, this option may be less constraining.

### Cash Purchase

Many foreign buyers (particularly in the diaspora) buy entirely in cash. This considerably simplifies the transaction and strengthens your negotiating position with the seller.

## The Ideal Financing Plan

1. Define your total budget (price + ancillary costs)
2. Calculate your available down payment
3. Contact 2-3 Israeli banks before searching for a property (not the other way around)
4. Obtain an agreement in principle before signing
5. Sign the preliminary contract with a financing contingency clause
